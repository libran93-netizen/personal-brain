# Decision: The 1:1 vetting call is a hard rule

**Context:** While building the field-leader onboarding skill, Karan walked through his real vetting sequence: detailed intake first (name, contact, location, experience, company registration, certificates, and the full range of activities a leader runs), then a personal conversation before anyone is onboarded.
**Decision:** Every prospective field leader gets a personal one-on-one call before onboarding. The call is never replaced by a form, at least in TON's early phase.
**Consequences:** Onboarding stays slower but personal — the call protects both the quality bar and the relationship-first character of the network. The onboarding skill encodes this so any future outreach or intake flow is built around the call, not instead of it. Profiles also capture a leader's complete activity range (courses, workshops, other adventure activities), not just a single "trek leader" box.
